Historical Places


Irrecha Place (Hora Arsadi)

Lake Hora is where the annual Ireecha being celebrated. Irreechaa is the annual Oromo people Thanksgiving Day that celebrated every year in Birraa near the river bank or water and tree. Irreechaa is celebrated every year in September in Bishoftu Hora Harsadi and other Oromia major cities. One of the distinct thanksgiving celebrations in the Oromo tradition is called “Irreechaa” or “Irreessaa,” which, by itself, means ‘green and fresh grass’ that symbolise fertility and flourishing life due to the bless and guidance of the Creator, Waaqaa.

Chukala Mount

Mount Zuqualla or Chuqqaala is an extinct volcano, it rises from the plain 30 km south of Bishoftu. With a height of 2,989 metres (9,806 ft), it is known for its crater lake, lake Dembel, an elliptical crater lake with a maximum diameter of about one kilometer, but the trail around the crater is about 6 kilometers long. The mountain is historical and attractive because of excellent panoramic view of the surrounding area and Church of Abo, an old monastery of over 500 years old, which has a repository of old manuscripts of religious significance written on well prepared goat hides known as ‘birana’.

Ethiopian Air Force

Ethiopian Air Force has a history which is tightly woven with the history of Bishoftu. It is established in 1946 following assigning of Bole Airport to Ethiopian Airlines, the civil Aviation of the country. The Air force base is constructed by Swedish Instructors and Ethiopian Cadets.The origins of the Ethiopian Air Force has been traced to Haile Selassie witnessing a show of the British Royal Air Force in November 1922, in Aden. Having never seen an airplane before, he was captivated by this demonstration of their power and abilities, and spontaneously asked if he could go up in one of the biplanes, proclaiming that it was "very fitting that he, as regent of Ethiopia should be the first Ethiopian to take flight in an aeroplane. As a result of this experience, he advocated the development of the Imperial Ethiopian Air Force.

Oda Nabe

In Oromo culture and history, the Odaa is regarded as a source of peace and a symbol of identity, unity of the Oromo people. Due to its religious and political importance, the Oda is the most revered and blessed of the trees. It is extremely long-lived. It blooms naturally and its appearance resembles a fig or a butterfly. This tree does not dry out even if the May is long. Besides, it grows naturally underground where there is water or moisture. Its growth is somewhat higher and its branches widen to flourish in waves every year. The Odaa shade is the place where the Muuda ceremony takes place and where the members of the Gada celebrate the transitional ceremony. The Abba Muuda hall and the seat of the Abbootii Gada are built under the Odaa.
